## Frequently asked

### What's the difference between Free and Pro?

Free is genuinely useful: unlimited treatment logging, unlimited daily
check-ins (shedding, itch, notes), full 3-angle photo capture, in-app
transplant milestone cards, side-by-side comparison for your first 3 sessions,
the last 3 sessions in the Journal timeline, adherence trend for the last
30 days, and 30-day photo reminder notifications. **Your photos are always
free to view — no paywall, ever.**

Pro adds unlimited side-by-side comparisons, the full photo history across all
sessions, the full adherence trend history, push notifications for transplant
milestones, PDF export for derm or clinic visits, and iCloud sync across your
Apple devices.

### Monthly or yearly?

Both unlock the same Pro features. The yearly plan works out to roughly
$3.33/month vs. $4.99 monthly, and it includes a 7-day free trial. You can
switch at any time in **Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions**.

### Does the app analyse my photos automatically or use AI?

No. There is no AI scan, no face recognition, and no server that processes
your images. The ghost overlay on the capture screen is a framing guide —
it shows your previous photo so you can match the angle. Nothing is uploaded
automatically.

### What are the transplant milestone cards?

If you enter a surgery date during onboarding, the app generates 9
rule-based milestone cards keyed off that date:

| Milestone | Days post-op |
|---|---|
| Rest period | Day 3 |
| Scabs clearing | Day 10 |
| Normal washing | Day 14 |
| Shock loss warning | Month 1 |
| The shed | Month 3 |
| First fine hairs | Month 4 |
| First major growth window | Month 6 |
| Thickening phase | Month 9 |
| Final result assessment | Month 12 |

The month-3 card ("the shed") is the most important one. Shock loss after
a transplant is normal and temporary — the card explains this calmly so you
know what to expect.

### My hair looks worse after the transplant — is that normal?

Yes, for most patients. Shock loss (temporary shedding of transplanted and
native hairs) typically peaks around weeks 4–8 and is a normal part of the
healing process. The app's month-3 milestone card covers this in detail.
Always defer to your clinic's protocol and consult your surgeon if you have
concerns.

### How often should I take progress photos?

Most users find weekly or every-2-days photos to be the right cadence — enough
to see gradual change without the overhead of daily captures. Set your photo
cadence in Settings and the app will remind you when one is due. The Journal
tab shows a dot when a photo is due.

### Where are my photos stored?

Locally on your device, in the app's private storage. They are never uploaded
to our servers, excluded from automatic cloud backups by default, and not
added to your camera roll. If you turn on iCloud sync (Pro), they are stored
in your private CloudKit container — we cannot access them.

### Can I use photos from my camera roll?

Yes. On the capture screen, tap the photo library icon in the bottom-left
corner to pick a photo from your library instead of taking a new one.

### How do I export data for my surgeon or dermatologist?

**Settings → Export PDF** generates a report covering your treatment
adherence, shedding and itch trends, and a photo session summary for the
date range you pick. (Pro feature.)

### How do I restore my purchase on a new phone?

Open **Settings → Restore purchases** (or the paywall → Restore). Make sure
you're signed in to the same Apple ID you used to buy the subscription.

### What happens if I downgrade from Pro to Free?

Nothing is deleted. All your photos, logs, check-ins, and milestone data
remain visible. iCloud sync is paused. New items beyond Free limits (more than
3 sessions for side-by-side) are gated until you upgrade.

### How do I cancel my subscription?

**Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions** on your iPhone, tap the Follicle
subscription, then **Cancel**. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the
current billing period.

### How do I delete my data?

**Settings → Privacy → Delete all data** wipes every log, photo, and
check-in on your device. Or delete the app — that removes local data too.

### How do I turn off notifications?

**Settings → Reminders** in the app. Each notification type (treatment
reminder, photo reminder, milestone push) has its own toggle.
